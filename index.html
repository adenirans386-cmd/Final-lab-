San Francisco Population Density Analysis

Problem Type: Demographic Analysis & Spatial Distribution

The Story

This map reveals the stark contrasts in how San Francisco's population is distributed across its neighborhoods. Using census tract data, I calculated population density to identify the most densely populated areas of the city. The analysis shows that certain neighborhoods have population densities exceeding 170,000 people per square mile, while others remain relatively sparse. This type of analysis is critical for urban planners making decisions about public transit routes, emergency service placement, and infrastructure investment. The graduated color scheme makes it immediately clear where the city's population pressure points are concentrated, particularly in the northeastern and central portions of San Francisco.

GIS Tools & Skills Used

Field Calculator (Density Calculation) Census Tract Analysis Choropleth Mapping Data Normalization Graduated Color Symbology Attribute Table Manipulation Spatial Join Operations

Philadelphia Gun Violence & Health Access Analysis

Problem Type: Spatial Join & Multi-layer Overlay Analysis

The Story

This map examines the intersection of public safety and health access in Philadelphia by overlaying gun violence incidents with the locations of healthy corner stores and transit routes. The spatial join analysis reveals patterns between neighborhoods experiencing gun violence and their access to health resources. The bright magenta area represents the combined spatial extent where these factors intersect, showing how communities facing violence may also lack convenient access to healthy food options. This type of analysis helps public health officials and community organizations understand how multiple social determinants of health overlap geographically, enabling more targeted intervention strategies in Philadelphia's most vulnerable neighborhoods.

GIS Tools & Skills Used

Spatial Join Analysis Multi-layer Overlay Point Data Mapping Polygon Dissolve Operations Network Data Integration (SEPTA) Map Layout & Legend Design Scale Bar & North Arrow

Philadelphia Regional Census Block Analysis

Problem Type: Multi-criteria Spatial Analysis & Regional Planning

The Story

This map presents a comprehensive regional view of Philadelphia and its surrounding counties using census block-level data. The vibrant color classification system (ranging from yellow to red to orange) reveals distinct spatial patterns across the metropolitan area. Unlike broader census tract analysis, census blocks provide the finest level of geographic detail available from the Census Bureau, enabling precise identification of demographic or land use characteristics. The analysis encompasses Philadelphia proper as well as neighboring jurisdictions including Pennsauken, Gloucester City, and surrounding communities in New Jersey. This type of fine-grained spatial analysis is essential for targeted policy interventions, resource allocation decisions, and understanding hyperlocal variations that might be masked by larger geographic units.

GIS Tools & Skills Used

Census Block Geography Multi-class Classification Regional Analysis (Multi-county) Graduated Color Symbology OBJECTID Field Management Cartographic Design Legend & Map Elements
